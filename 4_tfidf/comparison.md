When I classified the news stories with Bag of Words, I got an F1 score of 0.74. When I switched to TF-IDF, the F1 score dropped to 0.71. While TF-IDF typically yields a higher F1 score with logistic regression, that wasn't the case here. This could be because I used headlines to classify the articles, which are usually only a few words long. This likely increases the importance of the raw frequency of words, and less on words' relative frequency when compared to other headlines. My other thought is that this occurs because there are 18 classes, and headlines in each class may use similar words. Between a relatively high number of classes and TF-IDF devaluing some of the key words that would help classify articles, this could explain why the Bag of Words model gave me a higher F1 score.
