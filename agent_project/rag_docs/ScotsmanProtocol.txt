To: USU Spirit Squad, Marching Band, and Game Operations
From: Director of Game Day Atmosphere
Subject: Synchronization of "The Scotsman" Chant

We have noticed some timing inconsistencies during the performance of "The Scotsman" at recent basketball games. To ensure maximum impact, it is critical that the band director and the cheer captain coordinate the transition immediately following "Hail the Utah Aggies." The chant must begin precisely two beats after the fight song concludes. The synchronized arm gestures—mimicking the milking of a cow—must be uniform across the cheer team and the student section.

Please remind all spirit squad members that the tempo should not accelerate during the second verse. The crescendo should build naturally, culminating in the final shout of "Where the sagebrush grows!" The goal is to have the entire arena clapping in unison by the final measure. We will be scheduling a brief rehearsal with the pep band and the cheerleaders this Thursday evening in the Spectrum to iron out these timing issues before the rivalry game this weekend.
